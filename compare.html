TG is memory-bandwidth bound: each token reads the full model weights (~7.7 GB) plus accumulated KV cache. B70 Vulkan's 21 t/s vs 4080's 55 t/s reflects the BW difference (~512 vs ~400 GB/s dedicated, but CUDA paths are much more optimized). SYCL's 60 t/s activates full XMX (INT8 native), which is why it matches or exceeds the 4080.

⚠ SYCL bench-only — production blocked by commit 0988accf8 bug.

At pp512, the 4080 is actually slower than the 780M — CUDA has higher scheduling overhead on small batches. The 4080 shines at large batches: at pp8192 it reaches 6,343 t/s (+13× vs 780M). The B70's PP advantage at small batches comes from dedicated fast GDDR6 with no shared-memory contention.

The 26B-A4B model alone is 16 GB — it simply doesn't fit in the 4080's 12 GB VRAM. The 780M can load it into shared RAM but TG speed at 256K ctx would be severely limited by DDR5 bandwidth. B70 is the only GPU in this setup that runs 26B-A4B at 256K context with good TG speed.
